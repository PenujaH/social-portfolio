ISM Robotics Club

ISM Robotics Club was the first club I ever joined. Honestly, none of us knew much at the beginning—we were all complete newbies. But that’s what made it exciting.

We started with the basics: learning about Arduino and ESP32 microcontrollers. Slowly, it all began to click. Wires, sensors, and tiny circuits turned into something real.

Soon, we started building projects together. A remote-controlled Bluetooth car. Obstacle detection gadgets. Even a four-legged spider robot. They weren’t perfect, but they were ours—and we made them work as a team.

Through this, we learned more than just electronics and IoT. We learned how to start from zero, how to experiment, how to fail and try again.

Joining ISM Robotics Club showed me how much you can do with curiosity, teamwork, and a few lines of code.
